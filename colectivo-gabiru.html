Colectivo Gabirú
| FRESTA |


According to the dictionary, “fresta”, a portuguese word, is a narrow opening that allows the passage of light and air; a slit, gap, or simply a small opening in a wall or roof for ventilation and illumination. Contemplating these contexts, it is fair to add: “fresta” is also the gap that invites a curious peek and sharpens the imagination for discovery. Upon entering the Bairro do Rego for the first time, Colectivo Gabiru observed it with the surprise of a foreigner's senses. In an aimless route, its members gathered the specificities of the region and, in suppositions, created a scenario about the past and the near future. What would indicate the bustling movement of scaffolding between traditional constructions? Or the trace of noise left by tireless planes and trains? There are numerous questions that sparked the group's interest in the location. The Artistic Residence in Technological and Multimedia Arts of the Lisbon City Council facilitated the deepening of this initial contact and, by providing three months of studio space on Rua Augusto Abelaria for the conception of works and group meetings, consequently, brought them closer to the multiculturalism of its residents and the life of the neighborhood. “Fresta" is an exhibition by the Coletivo Gabiru, formed by Thaynã Targa, Pablo Diaz e Fabio Curi, the result of the Artistic Residence of Technological and Multimedia Arts - LAAR offered by the Câmara Municipal de Lisboa.
