RESEARCH


Part of my practice consists of systematizing the interaction with a particular material, thus attempting to give relevance to aspects and features that tend to be invisible, unknown, or irrelevant to other artists, craft people, scientists, etc. The frameworks incorporate and embed different techniques and logic tools to map, observe, and dialogue with materials and their materiality.

Raw Ceramics
| Enfoques y experiencias temporales y físico-sonoras con arcilla. Proyecto interdisciplinario sobre el trabajo sonoro desde y con la materia |

"The purpose of this work lies in addressing the sound quality of raw clay, that is, its nature. This is done intuitively, critically, proactively, and ideally empirically. It is a broad reflection on the material through its observation. To achieve this, experiences ranging from the most subjective to the most objective will be developed, with an effort made to document them."

Overfied Ceramics
| Observation of commercial red clay SIO2 Overfired Structures: Experimental overfired-ceramic fusing techniques applied to sound sculpture practices |


This multidisciplinary study explores material characterization and sound-signal analysis to observe overfired ceramic samples. It examines how temperature and phase changes affect the chemical, mechanical, and sound properties of ceramic bodies, with a focus on sound sculpture practices. The study aims to provide simple methodological techniques for this domain. Overfired ceramics, produced by excessive firing temperatures, often lack functionality and durability, making them unsuitable for conventional use but potentially valuable for sound art. The research uses commercial red clay to observe its stages and sound behavior, using standard shapes for analysis. It is structured into three stages: contextualizing the practice within sound-producing ceramics, experimental design with material characterization and sound activation, and exploring artistic applications based on experimental outputs. This study links material science, sound art, and sculpture, offering insights into the acoustic properties and artistic potential of overfired ceramics. It provides theoretical and practical applications for artists and ceramicists, emphasizing the intersections between materiality, sound, and artistic expression, and expanding the tools and techniques available for sound sculpture.
